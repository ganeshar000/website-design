Are you a home-gardener looking for a way to make your crop more eco-friendly?

Or maybe you want to start your own garden, and don’t know where to begin? Well, you have come to the right place! A Hydroponics system incorporates versatility, savings and eco-friendliness all into one. It is a method of growing soil-less plants and nutrients are given to plants through different mediums like perlite, rockwool, vermiculite, peat moss etc. Check out this video for more information:

Pros

You can grow your own food at more sustainable costs, and a backyard greenhouse can be turned into a profitable business for yourself!
It uses 20% less water than regular soil gardening, and water is conserved since system water is reused.
Its environmental sterile since pesticides aren’t used.
Plants can be grown year round, since it isn’t weather dependent.
There is no soil set-up and testing issues.
You can set this up in a limited space.

Cons

Hydroponics requires constant system monitoring, and a closer watch on your plants than regular gardening.
Finding local installers can be difficult
Doesn’t work for all types of roofs
It demands technical expertise, which comes with time.
The production of crops is often limited.
If a disease appears, all plants in the system are affected.
Depending on your system, it can be vulnerable to power outages.

Planning a system for yourself



When it comes to setting up a hydroponics system for yourself, there are many factors to take into consideration, like the space you have, your budget, what plants you want to grow etc. but the flexibility of these factors makes it really easy to customize to your needs. Learn more about the different types of systems by clicking here.

A Hydroponics system does not need to be a fancy, professional system bought online. You can DIY most of the items! But before you do, it is important to carefully research your options before you settle on buying or building. Check out this website which lists the items often needed in a system and how you can get them.

You can set up a hydroponics system anywhere from the backyard of your home, to your patio or even a basement, but ensure that the flooring is even so that the hydroponic structure you place is stable. Explore this website to find ideas that fit your space!

Costs

You can build/buy a system for $150 or less, and more upscale ones can cost around $400. This cost is minimal compared to the savings you will have with home grown herbs and vegetables, but check out this website that breaks down the cost for a hydroponics system, so you can plan accordingly!
