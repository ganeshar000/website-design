Are you the urban dweller who constantly dreams of growing their own fresh herbs and harvesting plump tomatoes, but don’t have the space to? The rooftop garden is a perfect solution for you! This method utilizes wasted space, while also adding a fun and decorative vibe! Click here to fund some design ideas to get you started!

Pros

Improves the drainage system and helps prevent flooding in urban areas
It reduces carbon dioxide emission while increasing oxygen levels.
It reduces heat emission in the building, lowering energy costs.
It conserves water by capturing and harvesting rainwater and also by preventing stormwater runoff.
It helps increase the lifetime of your roof by almost thrice as much.

Cons

They are slightly more expensive than traditional roofs.
It does require extra care and maintenance.
It doesn’t work for all types of roofs, since only some types can handle the weight load.
Installation and maintenance costs often lead to higher insurance payment for roof gardens.

Planning a system for yourself

There are two main types of gardens you could set up:
1. Container Gardens:

  • Easy to DIY! Use anything from old Tupperware boxes to wooden baskets as planters! Click here to see some fun ideas.
  • When considering what fits your space and budgets the best, check out your local gardening center first. Chances are, they have pre-made planters and can advise you on specifics like location, installations and free design tips! Click here to learn more about space and other installation conditions.

2. Green Roof Gardens:
  • For the gardener willing to go all out, consider the green roof garden. It is a more complex layout for gardening which involves converting the entire rooftop into a structural system which can support plant insulation, drainage and plant growth mediums.
  • Always contact a licensed professional to check out the structural integrity of your building first, and get the green signal to go green! Check out this website for more information on gardening laws and licenses you might require before implementing green roofs.

Costs

  • Container Gardens often cost less, often between $500-$1000 for installation and maintenance. For more budgeting tips, click here.
  • Green roof gardens are a little more expensive and can cost anywhere from $30 per sq.ft. to $200 per sq.ft, but if installed properly, can enhance the lifespan of a conventional roof by almost 20 years! Want more information on costs? Click here to find out.
