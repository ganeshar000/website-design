Pros

Lower electric bill by 50-90%
Renewable & clean energy
Turbines pay for themselves in a few years
Tax incentives are sometimes available from the government
You can get money from the power company if extra energy is produced from your wind system
Avoid high costs of utility power lines extended to remote locations
Can last up to 20 years, sometimes longer
Can be used for purposes such as water pumping on farms and ranches.

Cons

Annual maintenance
Initial costs are high
Need a good amount of land
Does not work for all locations
Turbines typically operate at 30% capacity
Can be damaged during lightning storms
Can kill birds

Cost

If you are looking for a smaller wind system, prices will probably be between $3,000-5,000 for every kilowatt (1,000 watts). If it will be a primary source of electricity (5-15kW), you can expect to pay $15,000-75,000. However, this is not the final cost since there are often federal/state incentives to take into account.

Zoning & Permitting Challenges

You may need to get a permit or zoning approval for your energy project. To learn more, click here.

Please remember that your area may have height or noise restrictions, so be sure to check that before investing and implementing a system. To do this, contact the local building inspector, board of supervisors, and/or planning board. Many concerns could be addressed by supplying objective data.
