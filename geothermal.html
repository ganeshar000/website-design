Try a Geothermal Heating and Cooling system for your home! Thousands of American homeowners have switched to cleaner and greener lives with this. Check out their stories here. Geothermal Heating and cooling works by using renewable energy stored in the ground as a heat source during winters and as a depository during summers. A series of pipes, known as an earth loop, is buried under the ground, which carries an eco-friendly solution of alcohol and water, allowing heat to move to and fro from the home. Learn more here

Pros

  • Most environmentally friendly heating and cooling system available
  • Doesn’t consume any fossil fuels
  • Doesn’t have a huge outdoor unit
  • Durable and dependable
  • 400% efficiency rating
  • Lasts twice as long as a traditional heating and cooling system
  • Much quieter
  • Lower carbon monoxide emissions make it safer for you and your family
  • EPA encourages installation of these systems, especially in highly favourable areas like the West Coast area. Check out the map here.

Cons

  • Site dependent installation: you’ll have to talk to an expert to know if your house is qualified
  • Payback from the system may take anywhere from 2-10 years: make sure you are on top of your finances when installing the system
  • Installation often disturbs surrounding landscapes: this only spans 2-3 days.

Costs

For a 2500 sq.ft. home, the cost can easily go up to $20,000 for a 60,000 thermal unit load. Although it has a pretty high cost, the utility bills go down by 40%-60%. Not only that, you also are eligible for 30% or more tax returns from the government. The overall savings and returns on a geothermal installation truly outweigh its initial installation cost.
