Professional Dress Code

    • Required for the following events: General Sessions, all Competitive Events (judged, non-judged, and open), Campaign Rally, Caucuses, Workshops, National Leadership Academy.
      • Women          
    • Pant or skirt suit.
    • Dress skirt or dress slacks with coordinated blouse and/or sweater.
    • Business professional “dress.”
    • BPA blazer with dress skirt or dress slacks and blouse or sweater.
    • Dress shoes or dress sandals with heel are required with all of the above.
    • Skirt length may be no greater than two inches above the top of the knee.
    • This excludes all denim for any of the above.
      		•
      Men    
      • Dress slacks, dress shirt, and tie.
      • Suit, dress shirt, and tie.
      • Sport coat, coordinated dress slacks, dress shirt, and tie.
      • BPA blazer with dress slacks, dress shirt, and tie.
      • Dress shoes and dress socks are required with all of the above.
      • This excludes all denim for any of the above.


    Casual Dress Code
    • Required for the following events: Tours, Special Events and Exhibit Hall.
      • Women    
    • Sportswear (jeans), pants, and shirt, T-shirt, and shorts.
    • Footwear required with all of the above.
      		•
      Men
      • Sportswear (jeans), pants and shirt, T-shirt, and shorts.
      • Footwear required with all of the above.
The following are unacceptable during BPA activities:
  • Strapless, spaghetti straps, tube tops, halter tops, midriff tops.
  • Spandex, lycra, or transparent clothing.
  • Cut-offs or ragged clothing.
  • Clothing with inappropriate words and pictures.
  • Swim suits must be covered with appropriate attire while in any area except the swimming pool or designated sunbathing area.

For more information about BPA, visit www.bpa.org

Transportation

Metro Train

  • Metro runs from run Monday – Thursday, 5 a.m. to 11:30 p.m.; Friday, 5 a.m. to 1 a.m.; Saturday, 7 a.m. to 1 a.m.; Sunday, 8 a.m. to 11 p.m.
  • During peak hours, most fares range from $2.25 to $6 per trip. During off-peak hours, fares typically range from $1.85 to $3.85.
  • You can find the metro map and other information at washington.org







Taxi

For taxis around the DC Metro area you can use the DC Rider App, it is available on both Iphone and Android.



Lyft and Uber

Both Lyft and Uber run in the DC metro area


Hop on Hop off Bus tours

  • For a more touristy way of travelling around DC, you could go on a hop on and hop off bus tour.
  • The bus is $50 for a day and there is free wifi on board
  • You can get more information on www.bigbustours.com


Bike Share

  • The Bike Share is run by Capital Bike Share
  • Pricing
    • A single trip under 30 mins is $2
    • A 24hr pass is $8; unlimited number of rides under 30 minutes
    • 3 day pass is $17; unlimited number of rides under 30 minutes

Lime and Bird Scooters

  • Bird Scooter prices are listed in the app

Attractions

WASHINGTON D.C. WEATHER

The White House

The White House is the official residence of the President. To schedule a pubic tour, you must request it in advance through your Congress Member. At the tour, if you are 18 years or older, you must have a valid photo ID. For more information, click here.
Hours of Operation: Tuesday through Saturday
Prohibited Items: Video cameras, Tablets, Weapons, Food, Liquids, Tobacco
Address: 1600 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington, DC 20500


The National Air and Space Museum

The National Air and Space Museum is the world's largest collection of aviation and space artifacts. Admission is free, and there are digital tours available for purchase. Also, there are virtual reality simulators to experience flying airplanes. For more information, click here.
Hours of Operation: 10:00 am to 5:30 pm Every Day
Prohibited Items: Weapons, Food, Drinks(Except bottled water)
Address: 600 Independence Ave SW, Washington, DC 20560



The Lincoln Memorial

The Lincoln Memorial is the most visited site in the National Mall, located on the western side. There is no fee for visiting the National Mall. There are guided tours available free of charge. For more information, click here.
Hours of Operation: Always Open
Address: 2 Lincoln Memorial Cir NW, Washington, DC 20037


The United States Capitol

The United States Capitol is one of the most recognizable symbols of representative democracy in the world. The guided tours do not need to be reserved in advance. The Capitol Visitor Center has many more activities for guests to enjoy. For more information, click here.
Hours of Operation: 8:30 am to 4:30 pm Monday through Saturday
Prohibited Items: Weapons, Food, Drinks, Aerosol containers
Address: First St SE, Washington, DC 20004


The National Zoological Park

The National Zoological Park is one of the oldest parks in the United States. The zoo aims to "provide engaging experiences with animals and create and share knowledge to save wildlife and habitats." The zoo has many different species, including giant pandas. For more information, click here.
Hours of Operation: 8:00 am to 5:00 pm Every Day
Address: 3001 Connecticut Ave NW, Washington, DC 20008
